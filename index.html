Mission

The Yawarra Cultural Custodians Council (YCCC) safeguards Indigenous sovereignty in every project. Our mission is to ensure that investment, enterprise, and development align with cultural law, Elder approvals, and intergenerational benefit. Through the Cultural Assurance Framework (CAF) and Free, Prior and Informed Consent (FPIC), we certify projects that protect Country, respect culture, and create long-term value for communities.

Vision

Our vision is a future where Indigenous sovereignty guides Australia’s transition to a just and sustainable economy. By protecting Country, embedding cultural authority, and ensuring intergenerational benefit, we create a nation where investment, infrastructure, and enterprise honour both culture and community.

Differentiators

  • Dual Rail Governance: Cultural sovereignty (ORIC, YCCC) alongside fiduciary governance (ASIC, AFSL). This ensures every decision meets both cultural law and corporate law.
  • Sovereign Certification: Our Cultural Assurance Framework (CAF) eliminates “black-cladding” risk by guaranteeing cultural legitimacy and Elder approval.
  • Scalable Platform: From Indigenous-majority funds (YCM, YARNT) to project-level SPVs, we provide a structure that adapts to both community and institutional partners.
  • Traceability & Audit: All approvals, certifications, and decisions are securely recorded with integrity controls, ensuring transparency for both community and investors.

Project Development & Accelerator

Support for Indigenous and aligned entrepreneurs across two governance rails: YCCC + CCCH (sovereign) and YCM (fund manager).

Overview

  1. Initial Screening: Mandate fit, counterparties, cultural map.
  2. Consent & Cultural Gate: Custodian engagement, consent documentation, cultural knowledge register.
  3. Risk & Alignment: Cultural, legal, regulatory and financial risks; benefits and equity pathways.
  4. Due Diligence: Technical, financial, legal and governance validation with community review loop.
  5. Investment Structuring: Finalise SPV/fund terms; embed benefit-sharing and reporting.

Project Screening (lite)

A concise, plain-English gate to confirm cultural, strategic and fiduciary fit before deeper work.

  • Mandate & thematic fit (energy, nature repair, water/waste, materials)
  • Cultural authority identified; consent pathway and custodians mapped
  • Counterparties & governance clarified
  • Stage & timing: readiness, permits, land access, critical path
  • Capital ask & use defined: amount, structure, deployment timeline
  • Data room readiness checked (financials, technical, legal, ESG)
  • Early risks logged (cultural, legal, regulatory, commercial)
  • Next step agreed: diligence scope, owners, and timeline

Cultural Assurance Framework (CAF)

The Cultural Assurance Framework operationalises FPIC, ICIP and benefit pathways. Projects clear these gateways prior to capital deployment.

  • FPIC: Free, Prior and Informed Consent via culturally legitimate processes; revocable until execution.
  • ICIP & Data Sovereignty: Custodian-governed knowledge, data and symbols reflected in contracting and reporting.
  • Equity & Benefits: Where appropriate, Indigenous ownership at manager/SPV level, revenue participation, jobs and procurement targets.
  • Dual Governance: ORIC (sovereign) with ASIC/AFSL (fiduciary) to avoid black-cladding.
  • Traceability: Version-controlled approvals and KPIs, audit-ready artifacts.
